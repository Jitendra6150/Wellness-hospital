DORMITORY

Our hospital dormitory is a large room or area within a hospital facility where patients stay temporarily for observation, rest, or treatment. It is typically used for patients who do not require intensive care but still need medical supervision or assistance.

In a hospital dormitory, patients may receive various treatments depending on their medical condition. Some common treatments and services that may be provided in a hospital dormitory include:

  • Medication administration
  • Monitoring vital signs
  • Rehabilitation therapy
  • Nutritional support
  • Symptom management
  • Patient education
  • Social support

SONOGRAM

A sonogram, also known as ultrasonography, is a medical imaging technique that uses high-frequency sound waves to produce images of the inside of the body. It is commonly used to visualize organs, tissues, and blood flow in real-time without the use.

During a sonogram, a small handheld device called a transducer is used to transmit high-frequency sound waves into the body. These sound waves bounce off internal structures and return to the transducer, creating echoes.

PULMONARY

it's a medical specialty focused on the diagnosis and treatment of diseases related to the respiratory system.These include asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), pneumonia, bronchitis, lung cancer, pulmonary fibrosis, and pulmonary embolism.

Pulmonary specialists use various diagnostic tools such as pulmonary function tests, chest X-rays, CT scans, bronchoscopy, and arterial blood gas analysis to evaluate lung function and identify respiratory conditions.Treatment for pulmonary conditions varies depending on the specific condition and its severity. It may include medications (such as bronchodilators, steroids, or antibiotics), oxygen therapy, pulmonary rehabilitation, lifestyle modifications, and, in some cases, surgical interventions like lung transplantation.

  • Pulmonary Medicine
  • Prevention and Management
  • Collaboration with Other Specialties
  • Diagnostic Tools

Overall, pulmonary medicine plays a critical role in maintaining respiratory health and treating conditions that affect the lungs and airways, ultimately improving patients' quality of life and overall well-being.
