DORMITORY

Our hospital dormitory is a large room or area within a hospital facility where patients stay temporarily for observation, rest, or treatment. It is typically used for patients who do not require intensive care but still need medical supervision or assistance.

In a hospital dormitory, patients may receive various treatments depending on their medical condition. Some common treatments and services that may be provided in a hospital dormitory include:

  • Monitoring vital signs

  • Nutritional support

SONOGRAM

A sonogram, also known as ultrasonography, is a medical imaging technique that uses high-frequency sound waves to produce images of the inside of the body. It is commonly used to visualize organs, tissues, and blood flow in real-time without the use.

During a sonogram, a small handheld device called a transducer is used to transmit high-frequency sound waves into the body. These sound waves bounce off internal structures and return to the transducer, creating echoes.
